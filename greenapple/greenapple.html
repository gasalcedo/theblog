Green Apple Books: small manga shelf, big bookstore heart

GS
Gabe S. · March 29, 2026 · 5 min read

Green Apple Books is the kind of place where you go in for one book and leave with three you did not know you needed. The Clement Street location is dense: tables, nooks, handwritten shelf talkers, and a rhythm that feels closer to a neighborhood institution than a chain endcap. Manga is not the whole store, but it is absolutely part of the personality.

When you enter the store, they have a strict bag drop off policy to ensure the safety of their customers and their products. The employees were so kind to take in both my backpack and my lunchbox!

The manga section is at the first floor so it is not that hard to find. Unfortunately, the manga section itself is not that huge and while it is compact, I feel like it contains a great selection of mainstream manga! You should not expect Kinokuniya-level depth of manga by any means, rather think of their collection as curations rather than exhaustive.

I was walking around the bookstore and I was astounded with the amount of books and genres they had in stock. It seemed like they had at least every genre that existed out in the reading world and the bookstore genuinely felt like a massive library!

For my USF readers out there, the commute to the Inner Richmond neighborhood is easy to justify if you already like used books, art tomes, or wandering. Just pair it with food on Clement, make an afternoon of it, and treat the manga shelf as a bonus round. It should not be your entire mission, unless you are chasing something specific...

What to look for

Indie stores rotate stock — these are the kinds of titles that often pop up in mixed manga/graphic novel bays.

Naruto

A dependable shonen staple that rotates in frequently.

Chainsaw Man

High-demand modern hit — worth checking volume gaps.

Tokyo Ghoul

Seinen classic with steady pull from returning readers.

Tips before you go

  • Best time to visit: weekday afternoons — quieter aisles
  • Prices: used can beat big-box; new is often cover price
  • Bring a tote — you will find something heavy
